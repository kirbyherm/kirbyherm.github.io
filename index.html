I am a Nuclear Astrophysics Ph.D. candidate at Michigan State University supported by the Joint Institute for Nuclear Astrophysics -- Center for the Evolution of the Elements. My expected graduation is Spring 2023 and I am interested in both industry and academic opportunities.

My research interests include Urca cooling in superbursting systems, nucleosynthesis in core-collape supernovae, and optimizing accelerator methods for nuclear astrophysics experiments.

A list of my scientific publications can be found here, and a brief description of my projects are available on my research page. For more information, please see my resume, and my advisor's group page.

Additionally, I am very interested in energy and climate policy, and ensuring responsible stewardship of nuclear weapons and waste.
